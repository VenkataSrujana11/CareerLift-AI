RESUME BEST PRACTICES AND ATS OPTIMIZATION GUIDE
================================================

SECTION 1: RESUME STRUCTURE
----------------------------
A strong resume follows this order:
1. Contact Information (name, email, phone, LinkedIn, GitHub/portfolio)
2. Professional Summary (2-4 sentences tailored to each role)
3. Technical Skills (categorized: Languages, Frameworks, Tools, Platforms)
4. Work Experience (reverse chronological, bullet points with metrics)
5. Projects (notable personal or academic projects with tech stack and impact)
6. Education (degree, institution, graduation year, GPA if above 3.5)
7. Certifications (AWS, Google Cloud, Kubernetes, etc.)
8. Achievements/Awards (hackathons, publications, patents)

SECTION 2: WRITING STRONG BULLET POINTS
-----------------------------------------
Use the format: [Action Verb] + [What you did] + [Impact/Result]
BAD: "Responsible for API development"
GOOD: "Designed and implemented REST APIs serving 2M+ daily requests, reducing average response time by 40%"

Strong action verbs: Architected, Designed, Developed, Implemented, Optimized, Reduced, Improved, Led, Launched, Automated, Migrated, Delivered, Built, Engineered, Created, Managed, Scaled, Integrated

Quantify everything possible:
- Team size you led/collaborated with
- Performance improvements (%, ms, latency)
- Scale (users, transactions, data volume)
- Cost savings ($, %)
- Time saved (hours, days)

SECTION 3: ATS OPTIMIZATION
-----------------------------
Applicant Tracking Systems scan resumes before humans do.

Key ATS rules:
1. Use standard section headings (not creative names)
2. Submit as PDF or DOCX (not JPG or fancy design formats)
3. Avoid tables, headers/footers, text boxes (ATS can't read them)
4. Use keywords from the job description verbatim
5. Spell out acronyms at least once
6. Use standard fonts: Calibri, Arial, Helvetica, Times New Roman

Keyword matching:
- Copy the job description and identify 10-15 key technical terms
- Ensure your resume uses those exact terms
- Tools: Jobscan, Resume Worded

SECTION 4: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
-------------------------------------
1. Typos and grammatical errors (use Grammarly)
2. Generic objective statements
3. Using "I" or "we" (use action verbs directly)
4. Including a photo (in the US/UK — legal risk for employers)
5. Including references ("Available upon request" is obsolete)
6. One resume for all jobs (tailor for each application)
7. Listing duties instead of achievements
8. Resume longer than 2 pages (for < 10 years experience)
9. Including irrelevant personal info (age, marital status)
10. Inconsistent formatting (dates, font sizes)

SECTION 5: LINKEDIN OPTIMIZATION
----------------------------------
1. Professional headshot (7x more profile views)
2. Headline: not just title, but value proposition
   Example: "Senior Software Engineer | Python | Distributed Systems | Building Scalable APIs"
3. About section: written in first person, 3-4 paragraphs, end with call to action
4. All LinkedIn experience sections should match resume
5. Request 3-5 specific skill endorsements
6. Get recommendations from managers/peers
7. Turn on "Open to Work" (if active job searching)
8. Engage: share articles, comment on posts, publish insights

SECTION 6: COVER LETTER TEMPLATE
-----------------------------------
[Date]
[Hiring Manager Name / "Hiring Team"]
[Company Name]

Dear [Name/Team],

Opening paragraph: Strong hook, mention the specific role and why you're excited about this company.

Body paragraph 1: Your most relevant experience/achievement that directly maps to their top requirement.

Body paragraph 2: A second relevant achievement demonstrating cultural/skills fit.

Closing paragraph: Clear call to action, express enthusiasm for next steps.

Regards,
[Your Name]
[Contact Info]

Keep it under one page. Customize for each application. Quality over quantity.

SECTION 7: INTERVIEW PREPARATION TIMELINE
-------------------------------------------
4 weeks before interview:
- Research company (products, culture, recent news)
- Review job description and map your experience to requirements
- Start coding practice (LeetCode, HackerRank)

2 weeks before:
- Prepare STAR stories (5-7 behavioral examples)
- Practice system design (for senior roles)
- Mock interviews with peers or AI tools

1 week before:
- Research your interviewers on LinkedIn
- Prepare smart questions to ask
- Review the company's tech stack and blog

Day before:
- Rest well — no cramming
- Prepare outfit, travel route, required documents
- Review your own resume thoroughly

Day of:
- Arrive / log in early
- Bring water and a notepad
- Breathe and be yourself
